# Warung Dedalu (Seminyak)
- **Description:** A relaxed, family-run warung offering authentic Balinese comfort food. Perfect for casual dining after a day at the beach.
- **Highlights:** Nasi goreng, nasi campur, chicken satay, vegetarian options.
- **Best For:** Budget-friendly meals and casual dining.
- **Location:** Jl. Petitenget No.235, Kerobokan Kelod 80361 Indonesia.
- **Seasonal Availability:** Year-round
- **Accessibiliy:** Wheelchair accessible seating available; outdoor seating with street views.
- **Peak Seasons:** Dry season (April – October).
- **Price Range:** Budget (IDR 20K–30K per dish, approx. 2 USD).
- **Activities:** Casual dining, enjoying local Balinese cuisine near Petitenget Beach.
- **Sustainability:** Locally sourced ingredients; eco-friendly waste management practices.
- **Rating:** 4.6/5

# Gong Jatiluwih (Tabanan)
- **Description:** Located near the Jatiluwih rice terraces, this warung offers stunning views of vibrant green landscapes alongside hearty Indonesian cuisine.
- **Highlights:** Indonesian buffet featuring tempeh rendang and chicken satay skewers; à la carte options available.
- **Best For:** Lunch with scenic views and traditional Balinese flavors.
- **Location:** Jl. Batu Luwih Kawan 43, Tabanan 80361 Indonesia.
- **Seasonal Availability:** Year-round
- **Accessibiliy:** Wheelchair accessible seating available; high tables with unobstructed terrace views.
- **Peak Seasons:** Dry season (April – October).
- **Price Range:** Budget to Mid-range (Buffet approx. 10 USD; à la carte dishes approx. 6 USD).
- **Activities:** Dining with panoramic views of rice terraces; exploring local flavors through buffet options.
- **Sustainability:** Partners with local farmers for fresh ingredients; promotes sustainable agriculture practices.
- **Rating:** 4.7/5

# Merah Putih (Seminyak)
- **Description:** An iconic fine dining restaurant celebrating Indonesia's finest cuisine and culture in a cathedral-like setting with teak pods and air-conditioned garden dining rooms.
- **Highlights:** Sumatran beef bak pao, crispy duck with balado sauce, tasting menu featuring traditional spices and creative fusions.
- **Best For:** Romantic dinners, social events, and elevated Indonesian dining experiences.
- **Location:** Jl Petitenget No. 100x, Seminyak, Bali.
- **Seasonal Availability:** Year-round
- **Accessibiliy:** Fully wheelchair accessible; private event spaces available for groups and celebrations.
- **Peak Seasons:** Year-round with increased demand during holiday seasons like Christmas and New Year's Eve.
- **Price Range:** Luxury (Rp 400K–550K per person).
- **Activities:** Fine dining, enjoying cocktails at the bar lounge, private events in an elegant setting.
- **Sustainability:** Focuses on locally sourced ingredients and energy-efficient architectural design for its dining spaces.
- **Rating:** 4.8/5

# Sangsaka (Seminyak)
- **Description:** An intimate restaurant in Seminyak offering modern Indonesian cuisine with bold flavors, creative dishes, and wood-fired cooking techniques.
- **Highlights:** Wagyu beef satay, Jimbaran BBQ scallops, snapper in banana leaf, and a nine-dish tasting menu over five courses.
- **Best For:** Foodies seeking innovative Indonesian cuisine and a sophisticated dining experience.
- **Location:** Jl Raya Pangkung Sari No. 100, Kerobokan Kelod, Kuta Utara, Bali.
- **Seasonal Availability:** Year-round
- **Accessibiliy:** Limited wheelchair access due to narrow entrance; reservations recommended for guaranteed seating.
- **Peak Seasons:** High demand during holiday seasons and weekends.
- **Price Range:** Mid-range to high-end (Rp 250K–Rp 500K per person).
- **Activities:** Fine dining, enjoying cocktails, and exploring modern takes on traditional Indonesian flavors.
- **Sustainability:** Focuses on locally sourced ingredients and sustainable wood-fire cooking techniques.
- **Rating:** 4.8/5

# Kayuputi (Nusa Dua)
- **Description:** A luxurious beachfront restaurant at The St. Regis Bali Resort offering Pan-Asian haute cuisine with ocean views and an award-winning wine list.
- **Highlights:** Eight-course degustation menu, Wagyu beef, Tasmanian salmon, and signature cocktails at the Champagne Bar.
- **Best For:** Romantic dinners, special occasions, and fine dining experiences by the beach.
- **Location:** The St. Regis Bali Resort, Kawasan Pariwisata Lot S6, Nusa Dua, Bali.
- **Seasonal Availability:** Year-round
- **Accessibiliy:** Fully wheelchair accessible; al fresco and indoor seating available with ocean views.
- **Peak Seasons:** High demand during holiday seasons like Christmas and New Year's Eve.
- **Price Range:** High-end dining (IDR 1,900K–IDR 2,500K per person for special menus).
- **Activities:** Fine dining with wine pairings, Sunday brunches, and beachfront celebrations.
- **Sustainability:** Uses local ingredients from land and sea; supports sustainable fishing practices.
- **Rating:** 4.9/5

# Kampoeng Kepiting (Crab Village) (Near Airport)
- **Description:** A no-frills seafood restaurant located in a mangrove forest near Ngurah Rai International Airport, offering fresh crab dishes and ecotourism experiences.
- **Highlights:** Kepiting Tol Bergoyang (crab in Balinese spices), grilled mackerel with turmeric marinade, fish head soup, and mangrove fruit salad.
- **Best For:** Seafood lovers and eco-conscious travelers seeking a unique dining experience near the airport.
- **Location:** Jl By Pass Ngurah Rai No. 1, Tuban, Kuta, Bali.
- **Seasonal Availability:** Year-round
- **Accessibiliy:** Wheelchair accessible via wooden bridges connecting bamboo gazebos; family-friendly seating available.
- **Peak Seasons:** Year-round with steady demand from domestic tourists and travelers transiting through the airport.
- **Price Range:** Budget-friendly (USD 5–10 per meal).
- **Activities:** Dining on fresh seafood, mangrove tours, canoeing, and learning about sustainable crab cultivation practices.
- **Sustainability:** Operated by local fishermen; supports mangrove conservation and sustainable crab farming techniques.
- **Rating:** 4.7/5
